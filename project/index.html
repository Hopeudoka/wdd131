Fun Facts

  • Formation and Growth: Ibiono Stake was officially organized on November 23, 2008, in Uyo, Akwa Ibom State, Nigeria. It has been an integral part of the rapid expansion of the Church in Nigeria and the surrounding West African region.
  • Strong Community Impact: This stake has played a significant role in community service and welfare projects, emphasizing the Church’s commitment to self-reliance, education, and providing clean water, healthcare, and humanitarian aid to local communities.
  • High Member Participation: The Ibiono Stake is known for its active membership participation. Members regularly engage in activities such as service projects, youth programs, and family-focused gatherings, strengthening bonds within the congregation.
  • Temple Affiliation: Members of the Ibiono Stake are currently served by the Aba Nigeria Temple, the only operational temple in Nigeria, located in Abia State. This temple provides members opportunities for worship and temple ordinances.
  • Significant Role in Church Missions: The Ibiono Stake supports missionary efforts across the region, with many members serving missions both locally and internationally. The Stake’s support reflects the dedication and growth of the Nigerian Saints within the global Church.
